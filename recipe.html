How to prepare Efo riro

Origin

Efo riro (Yoruba: ẹ̀fọ́ riro) is a vegetable soup and a native soup of the Yoruba people of western Nigeria. It is commonly also refered to as efo elemi meje (Vegetable with 7 lives) because of its richness in proteins. The two vegetables most commonly used to prepare the soup are Celosia argentea (ṣọkọ̀ yòkòtò) and Amaranthus hybridus (ẹ̀fọ́ tẹ̀tẹ̀). Ẹ̀fọ́ riro is often eaten with Nigerian swallow foods like eba, pounded yam, or fufu. Other starchy foods such as rice and boiled plantain may also be eaten with it.

What do you need to prepare ẹ̀fọ́ riro?

Ingredient Measurement
Efo leaves 1 bunch
Palm oil 1 cup
Pepper 2 cups
Tomatoes 1/2 cup
Lucost beans 2 Tbs
Shredded Onion 1 cups
Smoked fish 2 cups
Parboiled meat 2 cups
salt 1/2 Tbs
Maggi 4 cubes

Directions

  1. Blend all pepper, tomatoes and onions
  2. Heat up palm oil
  3. Pour in your blended pepper and let it cook for 20 minutes
  4. Pour in your locust beans
  5. Add your salt and Maggi. Ensure to taste
  6. Add your parboiled meat and smoked fish, and let it cook for another 10 minutes to ensure the pepper gets into the proteins
  7. Lastly, add your throughly washed efo and let it steam for 5 minutes
